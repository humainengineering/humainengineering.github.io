⚠️ MDMT Verification Required — Competent Engineer Sign-Off
The Minimum Design Metal Temperature (MDMT) of all pressure-containing components must be verified against the anticipated metal temperature on the day of test before proceeding. Metal temperature must remain above MDMT throughout the duration of the test. For carbon steel and low alloy steel, wall thickness governs MDMT per ASME VIII Div.1 Fig. UCS-66. This assessment must be reviewed and confirmed in writing by a competent engineer prior to test execution. This calculator does not perform MDMT verification.
☁️ Asphyxiation Hazard — Nitrogen (N₂)
N₂ is colourless, odourless, and gives no physiological warning before incapacitation. Oxygen concentration in the test area must be continuously monitored (minimum 19.5% v/v). Confined space entry procedures, permit-to-work, and atmospheric testing must be in place before personnel access. Ensure adequate forced ventilation. This risk is not quantified by this calculator.
🔥 Flammable Atmosphere Risk — Dry Air
Do not introduce air into any system that cannot be verified as free of hydrocarbons. Introduction of air into a hydrocarbon-contaminated system may create an explosive mixture. PCC-2 §501-6.2(b): Nitrogen is the preferred test medium. Gas-free certificate required prior to testing with air. This risk is not quantified by this calculator.

⚠ Scope & Limitations — Read Before Use
1. Conservative upper bound: This calculator assumes instantaneous total failure releasing 100% of stored energy. Actual energy release depends on failure mode, wall thickness, material ductility, restraint conditions, and system geometry. Results are a conservative screening estimate only.

2. System design not considered: Vessel geometry, nozzle orientation, pipe support/restraint, connected volume contribution, and pressure regulation are not accounted for in the stored energy equations.

3. TNT equivalence model: PCC-2 Eq. II-3 applies a single conversion factor (4,266,920 J/kg). No yield factor is applied — this is the standard's conservative approach for screening. For detailed consequence modelling use DNV PHAST, SAFETI or equivalent QRA tools.

4. Fragment table basis: Table 501-III-2-1 is based on the American Table of Distances (IME SLP-2-2011) for unbarricaded inhabited buildings. Results are conservative for open industrial sites with no personnel in direct line of sight.

5. Not a substitute for QRA: This tool is a code-based screening calculator. Where stored energy is significant, a detailed Quantitative Risk Assessment (QRA) and formal Hazard Analysis per PCC-2 §501-6.2(g) and Mandatory Appendix 501-IV must be performed by a competent process safety engineer.

6. Purging / inerting use: When used for purging risk assessment, this tool applies engineering judgement beyond the formal scope of PCC-2 Article 501 (which is written for pressure testing). Results should be treated as indicative only and reviewed by a competent engineer.

Reference: ASME PCC-2-2022, Article 501, Mandatory Appendix 501-II & 501-III. ASME B31.3-2024, §345.5.
